Teams and players form the foundation of CricPR's cricket management system. This guide explains how teams are organized, how players are managed, and how roles are assigned.

## Teams

Teams represent permanent cricket organizations with a stable roster of players.

### Team structure

Each team contains:

- **Name**: Unique identifier for the team
- **Location**: Geographic location of the team
- **Logo**: Optional team branding image
- **Players**: Array of player references
- **Captain**: Designated team leader (player reference)
- **Created by**: Player who created the team

<Warning>
Team names must be unique across the entire CricPR system.
</Warning>

### Team roster management

Teams maintain a permanent roster of players who:

- Can participate in any match for that team
- Are displayed in team profile pages
- Appear in search results when users look for teams
- Have historical statistics associated with the team

## Players

Players represent individual cricketers who can participate in matches.

### Player types

CricPR supports two types of players:

#### Regular players

- **Have user accounts**: Linked via `userId` field
- **Searchable**: Appear in player search results
- **Persistent**: Maintain statistics across matches
- **Can be**: Captains, scorers, or wicket keepers
- **Appear in**: Team rosters and match listings

#### Guest players

- **No user accounts**: `userId` is `null`
- **Not searchable**: Excluded from player search
- **Match-specific**: Created for individual matches only
- **Cannot be**: Captains or scorers (but can be wicket keepers)
- **Do not appear in**: Team rosters or general player listings

See the [Guest players](/concepts/guest-players) guide for detailed information.

### Player profile

Regular player profiles include:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `userId` | Reference to the user account |
| `shortName` | Display name for the player |
| `battingStyle` | Right, left, or none |
| `bowlingStyle` | Type of bowling (fast, spin, etc.) |
| `playingRole` | All-rounder, batsman, bowler, etc. |
| `isVerified` | Whether the player profile is verified |
| `status` | Current account status |

### Player statuses

Players can have the following statuses:

- **active**: Player is active and can participate in matches
- **incomplete_profile**: Player account exists but profile needs completion
- **deactivated**: Player has temporarily deactivated their account
- **deleted**: Player account has been deleted

<Info>
Most queries filter out players with `deleted` status automatically.
</Info>

### Batting styles

Players specify their batting hand:

- Right-handed
- Left-handed  
- None (for bowlers who rarely bat)

### Bowling styles

Players can select from various bowling styles:

- Right-arm fast/medium
- Left-arm fast/medium
- Right-arm spin (off-spin, leg-spin)
- Left-arm spin (orthodox, chinaman)
- None (for specialist batsmen)

## Team instances

While teams represent permanent organizations, **team instances** are match-specific configurations.

### Why team instances?

Team instances allow:

- Different lineups for different matches
- Guest players in specific matches
- Rotating captains and wicket keepers
- Historical accuracy (preserving match-day rosters)

### Team instance structure

Each team instance contains:

```javascript
{
  teamId: ObjectId,           // Reference to the permanent team
  players: [ObjectId],        // Match-day squad (regular + guest players)
  captainId: ObjectId,        // Match captain (must be regular player)
  wicketKeeperId: ObjectId,   // Match wicket keeper (can be guest)
  scorerId: ObjectId          // Match scorer (must be regular player)
}
```

<Check>
Captains and scorers must be regular players, not guest players.
</Check>

## Player roles in matches

When teams register for a match, specific roles must be assigned:

### Captain

- **Required**: Every team must have a captain
- **Must be**: A regular player (not a guest)
- **Responsibilities**: Team leadership, toss decisions
- **Reference**: Stored in team instance's `captainId`

### Scorer

- **Required**: Every team must designate a scorer
- **Must be**: A regular player (not a guest)
- **Responsibilities**: Recording match events and statistics
- **Reference**: Can be stored at match or team instance level

### Wicket keeper

- **Optional**: Not required but recommended
- **Can be**: Regular player or guest player
- **Responsibilities**: Fielding behind the stumps
- **Reference**: Stored in team instance's `wicketKeeperId`

<Tip>
The wicket keeper is the only role that can be assigned to a guest player.
</Tip>

## Player search and discovery

The system provides player search functionality:

- **Includes**: Regular players with user accounts
- **Excludes**: Guest players (no `userId`)
- **Filters**: By status (excludes deleted players)
- **Results**: Player profiles with statistics

## Team creation and ownership

When creating a team:

1. A player creates the team (recorded in `createdBy`)
2. Team name is validated for uniqueness
3. Initial roster can include the creator and other players
4. A captain can be designated (optional at creation)
5. Team location must be specified
