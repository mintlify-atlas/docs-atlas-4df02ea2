Tournaments organize multiple teams into structured competitions with various formats. CricPR supports several tournament formats and provides comprehensive management features.

## Tournament structure

A tournament consists of:

- **Basic information**: Name, description, city, dates
- **Visual assets**: Cover image and banner
- **Team management**: Team registration and capacity tracking
- **Format configuration**: Competition structure (round robin, knockout, etc.)
- **Organizer**: User or player who created the tournament
- **Status tracking**: Current state of the tournament
- **Entry details**: Registration deadline, entry fee, and prizes

## Tournament formats

CricPR supports four tournament formats:

### Round robin

Every team plays against every other team at least once.

- **Best for**: Small to medium tournaments (4-8 teams)
- **Ensures**: All teams get equal opportunities
- **Determines winner by**: Points table standings

### Knockout

Single-elimination format where losing teams are immediately eliminated.

- **Best for**: Large tournaments or time constraints
- **Ensures**: Quick progression to finals
- **Determines winner by**: Final match result

### Hybrid

Combines group stages (round robin) with knockout playoffs.

- **Best for**: Large tournaments (16+ teams)
- **Ensures**: Balance between fairness and excitement
- **Determines winner by**: Knockout playoffs after group stage

### Point based

Custom point allocation system across multiple match stages.

- **Best for**: Unique tournament structures
- **Ensures**: Flexible competition design
- **Determines winner by**: Total accumulated points

<Tip>
Choose your tournament format based on the number of teams, available time, and desired competition structure.
</Tip>

## Tournament statuses

Tournaments progress through different stages:

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| `upcoming` | Tournament is scheduled, teams can register |
| `ongoing` | Tournament has started, matches in progress |
| `completed` | All matches finished, winner determined |
| `cancelled` | Tournament was cancelled |

## Team registration

Tournaments manage team participation through:

- **Maximum teams**: Set capacity for the tournament
- **Current teams count**: Automatic tracking of registered teams
- **Team IDs**: Array of registered team references
- **Registration deadline**: Cut-off date for team sign-ups
- **Entry fee**: Optional fee for participation

### Registration constraints

<Warning>
Once a tournament reaches `maxTeams`, no additional teams can register.
</Warning>

The system automatically:
- Increments `currentTeamsCount` when teams join
- Validates against `maxTeams` before allowing registration
- Prevents duplicate team registrations

## Visibility and access

Tournaments can be:

- **Public** (`isPublic: true`): Visible to all users, open registration
- **Private** (`isPublic: false`): Invitation-only, restricted visibility

## Tournament organizers

Tournaments support two organizer types:

1. **User organizers**: Regular users managing tournaments
2. **Player organizers**: Players organizing competitions

The `organizerType` field determines which collection the `organizerId` references.

## Winner determination

Once a tournament completes:

- The winning team is recorded in `winnerTeamId`
- Winner is determined based on the tournament format
- Status changes to `completed`

## Date validation

The system enforces:

<Check>
`endDate` must be after `startDate`
</Check>

This validation runs automatically before saving tournament documents.

## Tournament matches

Matches within tournaments:

- Have `matchType` set to `"tournament"`
- Are linked to the tournament structure
- Contribute to points tables and standings
- Follow the tournament's format rules

## Prizes and incentives

Tournaments can specify:

- **Prize**: Description of rewards (text field)
- **Entry fee**: Amount required to participate (numeric, minimum 0)

These fields help organizers communicate tournament value to potential participants.
